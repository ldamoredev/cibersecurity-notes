Public Asset Discovery

Definition

Public asset discovery is the process of identifying internet-facing domains, subdomains, hosts, services, APIs, storage surfaces, and supporting infrastructure that belong to or materially relate to a target.

Why it matters

Before testing anything deeply, you need to know what exists. Public asset discovery is one of the fastest ways to reveal undocumented, legacy, or weakly governed exposure. This note stays focused on finding public-facing assets themselves, not proving ownership relationships like company-mapping or inferring implementation details like tech-stack-fingerprinting.

Attacker perspective

Attackers look for: - domains and subdomains - cloud-hosted entry points - public APIs - storage buckets and artifact paths - externally reachable admin or support surfaces

Defender perspective

Defenders should: - maintain visibility into internet-facing assets - identify ownership gaps - review which assets remain reachable but unsupported - feed discovery results into attack surface mapping and retirement

Practical examples

  • a forgotten static site still serves content
  • a public API host is reachable but absent from formal docs
  • public assets spread across multiple vendors and providers
